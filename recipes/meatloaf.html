Classic Meatloaf

This old-school meatloaf recipe is comfort food at its finest. It's moist, flavorful, and topped with a tangy ketchup glaze — just like grandma used to make back in the days of dial-up internet and VHS tapes.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ lbs ground beef
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ cup ketchup (for glaze)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar (for glaze)
  • 1 tbsp mustard (for glaze)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, onion, garlic, egg, milk, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper.
  3. Mix everything together until well combined, but don’t overmix or it’ll get tough.
  4. Shape the mixture into a loaf and place it in a baking dish or loaf pan.
  5. In a small bowl, mix ketchup, brown sugar, and mustard to make the glaze.
  6. Spread the glaze evenly over the top of the meatloaf.
  7. Bake uncovered for about 55–60 minutes, or until internal temp hits 160°F (71°C).
  8. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with mashed potatoes and veggies for full 2000s sitcom family dinner vibes.
